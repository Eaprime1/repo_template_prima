# Chambers

A chamber is what happens when a THEE door has been walked through.

Not all fragments become chambers.  
Most land in `incoming.md` and wait — warm, unnamed, patient.  
A chamber forms when the unknown is *entered*, not just received.

---

## What Makes a Chamber

- A name (even a temporary one)
- A date of entry
- At least one thing that changed when you arrived
- At least one door still open

Chambers are not rooms you design.  
They are rooms you discover you are already in.

---

## The Law of Chambers

**A chamber with no open doors is a room, not a chamber.**

Every chamber must leave one question unanswered.  
That question is the next door.  
That door is labeled THE.

---

## The First Chamber

`chambers/the-the/` — the mystery of THE THE

The founding chamber. The door about the door.  
All other chambers inherit from this one.

---

## Creating a Chamber

When a fragment in `incoming.md` becomes something you have entered:

1. Create `intake/chambers/[name]/README.md`
2. Describe what the chamber *is* — not what it will be
3. Note the date of entry and prime state
4. Leave at least one open door (one unanswered question)
5. Run `bash tools/thee.sh "[name] chamber opened"` to log it

```
chambers/
  the-the/          ← first chamber
  [your-concept]/   ← chambers grow here
```
